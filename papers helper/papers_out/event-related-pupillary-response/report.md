### 1. Pupillary responses and event‐related potentials as indices of the orienting reflex
_Genevieve Z. Steiner, Robert J. Barry · 2011 · Psychophysiology · source: OpenAlex_
**Relevance:** 9.0 / 10
**Summary.** This research investigated the physiological responses associated with the orienting reflex, specifically examining skin conductance, event-related potentials, and pupillary dilation. Contrary to previous assumptions, the study found that pupillary dilation primarily reflected novelty rather than the overall orienting response.  Instead, the skin conductance response and event-related potentials reliably indicated the orienting reflex, suggesting a more nuanced understanding of the physiological mechanisms underlying this reflexive behavior.
- **Method:** Auditory dishabituation paradigm; skin conductance response, event-related potential (late positive complex), pupillary dilation measurements
- **Dataset:** 24 participants
- **Results:** Skin conductance response and late positive complex are indices of the OR. Pupillary dilation sensitive to novelty; prestimulus pupil diameter showed significance effect.
- Link: <https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1469-8986.2011.01271.x>

### 2. Infrared webcam-based non-contact measurement of event-related potentials from event-related pupillary responses: An approach focused on mental workload
_Sangin Park, Laehyun Kim, Jihyeon Ha, Sungchul Mun · 2022 · Journal of Computational Design and Engineering · source: OpenAlex_
**Relevance:** 9.0 / 10
**Summary.** Researchers developed a new, non-contact method for measuring event-related potentials (ERPs) using an infrared webcam and tracking event-related pupillary responses.  The study, involving 32 participants performing mental arithmetic tasks, demonstrated significant differences in both ERP and ErPR data across varying levels of mental workload.  The technique showed strong correlations between the two measurements and achieved high classification accuracy, suggesting a promising, low-cost approach for monitoring ERP components.
- **Method:** Infrared webcam-based measurement of event-related pupillary responses (ErPRs) to determine event-related potentials (ERPs)
- **Dataset:** 32 healthy volunteers performing mental arithmetic tasks at three levels of mental workload
- **Results:** Statistically significant differences in ERP and ErPR amplitudes and latencies across MWL states. Classification performances of 73% and 80% using k-nearest neighbour (10-fold cross validation, n = 96). Correlation coefficients between ERP and ErPR features in the range of 0.642–0.734. Good agreement between indices in Bland–Altman plots.
- Link: <https://doi.org/10.1093/jcde/qwac059>
- PDF: <https://academic.oup.com/jcde/article-pdf/9/4/1327/45630655/qwac059.pdf>

### 3. Event-related pupillary response-based authentication system using eye-tracker add-on augmented reality glasses for individual identification
_Sangin Park, Jihyeon Ha, Laehyun Kim · 2024 · Frontiers in Physiology · source: OpenAlex_
**Relevance:** 9.0 / 10
**Summary.** Researchers developed a novel authentication system utilizing event-related pupillary responses (ErPR) captured through augmented reality glasses.  Comparing ErPR data with event-related potentials, they found significantly larger responses to familiar faces, allowing for highly accurate identification using a support vector machine.  This non-contact system, employing ErPR data within an AR environment, demonstrated high accuracy and reliability for individual identification.
- **Method:** Event-related pupillary response (ErPR) analysis combined with linear support vector machine and quadratic discriminant analysis classifiers.
- **Dataset:** Rapid serial visual presentation of familiar and unknown human photographs.
- **Results:** ErPR amplitudes for familiar faces were significantly larger than for stranger faces. ERP-based authentication achieved perfect accuracy. ErPR-based authentication achieved 97% accuracy with low false acceptance and rejection rates. Correlation coefficients between ERP and ErPR were 0.452-0.829.
- Link: <https://doi.org/10.3389/fphys.2024.1325784>
- PDF: <https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/physiology/articles/10.3389/fphys.2024.1325784/pdf>

### 4. The impact of personal relevance on emotion processing: evidence from event-related potentials and pupillary responses
_Mareike Bayer, Katja Ruthmann, Annekathrin Schacht · 2017 · Social Cognitive and Affective Neuroscience · source: OpenAlex_
**Relevance:** 9.0 / 10
**Summary.** This study examined how personal relevance affects our emotional responses when reading. Researchers found that words related to significant others triggered greater activity in the visual cortex and increased the amplitude of the Late Positive Complex (LPC) in participants’ brains.  Furthermore, personal relevance led to heightened attention and arousal, as measured by pupillary responses and ratings, and prolonged emotional responses in the brain’s visual and prefrontal regions.
- **Method:** Event-related potentials (ERPs) and pupillary responses
- **Dataset:** Emotional words presented in personal and unknown contexts
- **Results:** Personal relevance increased visual cortex activity (100ms), LPC amplitudes, pupillary responses, arousal ratings, and prolonged negative word ERP effects (200ms). Activations in prefrontal regions, visual cortex, and fusiform gyrus.
- Link: <https://doi.org/10.1093/scan/nsx075>
- PDF: <https://academic.oup.com/scan/article-pdf/12/9/1470/27104841/nsx075.pdf>

**Relevance:** 9.0 / 10
**Summary.** This study investigated how aging affects cognitive processes by examining brain activity in young and older adults performing a reaction time task. Researchers found that older adults exhibited increased cortical activation during the preparation phase of the task, indicated by specific brainwave patterns, but this was not reflected in changes to the activity of their noradrenergic system, measured through pupillary responses.
