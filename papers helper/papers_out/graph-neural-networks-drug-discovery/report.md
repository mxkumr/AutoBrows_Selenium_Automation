### 1. Could graph neural networks learn better molecular representation for drug discovery? A comparison study of descriptor-based and graph-based models
_Dejun Jiang, Zhenhua Wu, Chang‐Yu Hsieh, Guangyong Chen, Ben Liao, Zhe Wang et al. · 2021 · Journal of Cheminformatics · source: OpenAlex_
**Relevance:** 9.0 / 10
**Summary.** This research compared graph neural networks and traditional descriptor-based models for predicting molecular properties, utilizing 11 public datasets.  The study found that descriptor-based models, particularly SVM, generally outperformed graph-based models in terms of prediction accuracy and computational speed. Despite this, some graph-based models showed promising results, especially on larger datasets, and the research highlighted the interpretability advantages of descriptor-based models.
- **Method:** Graph Neural Networks (GNN), SVM, XGBoost, RF, DNN, GCN, GAT, MPNN, Attentive FP
- **Dataset:** 11 public datasets covering various property endpoints
- **Results:** Descriptor-based models outperform graph-based models in prediction accuracy and computational efficiency. SVM best for regression. RF and XGBoost reliable for classification. Attentive FP and GCN achieve outstanding performance on some datasets. XGBoost and RF most efficient.
- Link: <https://doi.org/10.1186/s13321-020-00479-8>
- PDF: <https://jcheminf.biomedcentral.com/track/pdf/10.1186/s13321-020-00479-8>
